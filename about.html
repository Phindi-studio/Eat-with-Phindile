About the Author

Phindile is the voice behind Eat With Phindile — a South African food blog that celebrates simple meals, honest reviews, and the joy of sharing food. She believes that every dish tells a story, whether it’s cooked at home or discovered in a hidden restaurant gem.

Sharing food, stories, and simple meals for everyday life

Eat With Phindile is a South African food blog for people who love real, accessible cooking — the kind that brings friends and families together. You’ll find honest restaurant reviews, easy recipes, and everyday ideas to make food a source of connection and joy.

How It All Started

My love for food began online, watching people review restaurants and recreate dishes at home. The colors, smells, and creativity always fascinated me. One day, my sister suggested we start cooking our favorite Friday takeout meals ourselves. What began as a way to save money turned into a weekly tradition — full of laughter, experiments, and shared memories.

Cooking together every Friday became our way to connect, laugh, and create memories.

Why Food Matters

Food, to me, is more than what’s on the plate. It’s how people come together — whether over homemade dishes or discovering new restaurants. Cooking with my sister reminded me that food connects us in ways social media or screens can’t. It brings life and laughter back into the room.

Food isn’t just about taste — it’s a bridge between people, cultures, and experiences.

What You’ll Find Here

On this blog, I share honest restaurant reviews, meal challenges, and practical food hacks. I also explore how people can earn through food — from cooking and selling meals to creating food content. My goal is to make food exciting, accessible, and useful for anyone who loves to eat and share.

My Approach

Unlike blogs that focus only on high-end restaurants, Eat With Phindile celebrates everyday food. I feature township gems, affordable spots, and recipes that anyone can try at home. It’s about keeping food real, relatable, and rooted in community.

Let’s Connect

I hope Eat With Phindile inspires you to cook more, explore more, and share your food stories. Try the recipes, read the reviews, and tell me what you think — I’d love to hear from you.
